COMPUTER MOTION LAUNCHES AESOP WORLD FIRST FDA CLEARED SURGICAL ROBOT CAPABLE OF UNDERSTANDING VOICE COMMANDS IS NOW AVAILABLE San Francisco CA October -- At the American College of Surgeons nd Clinical Congress Computer Motion the world leading supplier of medical robots announced the release of its next generation surgical robot -- AESOP with Voice Control. AESOP features the most intuitive interface available the ability to understand surgeon voice commands. This allows the surgeon to use spoken English to precisely maneuver and position laparoscope specially designed optical tube which when connected to medical video camera and light source is passed into the body to allow the surgeon to view the operation on video monitor AESOP pronounced eesop is the second generation of the company flagship product and was cleared by the FDA in May clinical advisory team comprised of leading endoscopic surgeons has worked closely with the company throughout the development of the surgical robot voice control interface. Clinical validations have been successfully completed at over institutions across the nation. Surgeons have been asking for an easy way to control their field of vision during surgery. Our research has confirmed that voice control is the ideal interface. We are delighted to offer this advanced solution today said Gene Wang CEO President of Computer Motion. We plan to apply our voice control technology across broad range of surgical devices to create an industry standard surgeon-to-machine interface for the operating room.

Designing protection Designing protection By Rachel Powell Norton December p.m. PT Digital watermarking is popular new way to protect copyrighted images. Here how it works Step Watermarkcreated The image creator stamps the image with digital watermark that can only be viewed with special software. The watermark takes advantage of the variation between pixels to remain all but invisible in the finished product. The watermark can still be detected with the special software even after the image is printed and rescanned. Step Watermark detected Image editors such as Photoshop are equipped with the special software. The application can detect the subtle pixel variation and interprets it as watermark alerting the viewer that the image has been copyrighted. Step Artist identified In addition to marking the image as copyrighted work the watermark contains information on how to contact the artist. Source PictureMarc Software Copyright CNET Inc.

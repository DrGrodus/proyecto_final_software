CPJ News Alert News Alert Index CPJ Website For Immediate Release October Contact Kakuna Kerina U.S. State Department Institutes Policy Change Toward Ethiopian Press During Christopher Visit to Addis Ababa Policy Change Enacted After Review of CPJ Report Revealing More Journalists Imprisoned in Ethiopia in Past Three Years Than in Any Other African Country New York U.S. Secretary of State Warren Christopher today declined the Ethiopian government invitation to hold joint press conference upon his arrival in Addis Ababa. This in response to report by the New York-based Committee to Protect Journalists CPJ detailing the legal harassment and imprisonment of Ethiopian journalists and the government systematic exclusion of the private press from official press conferences. State Department Spokesperson Nicholas Burns told the Committee chair Kati Marton that CPJ report Clampdown in Addis Ethiopia Journalists at Risk led to the policy change. In public statement Secretary of State Christopher told reporters Ethiopia has made progress in human rights during the past five years but the United States wants to see more. One of the areas of our concern is the freedom of the press and the treatment of journalists. CPJ report is based on fact-finding mission conducted last May by Africa Program Coordinator Kakuna Kerina who wrote the report and CPJ board member and Newsday U.N. bureau chief Josh Friedman who wrote the report introductory essay. Based on more than interviews with government officials journalists and other sources the report demonstrates how independent journalists are regularly harassed censored and jailed under the provisions of restrictive press law enacted in The repressive climate exists despite promises of press freedom made by the Transitional Government of Ethiopia TGE in and by the recently elected Prime Minister Meles Zenawi who served as TGE president. Clampdown in Addis which was released on October -- the day Ethiopia Parliament reconvened to address key media issues -- made several recommendations to the Meles administration and called on the United States and other Western countries to take more aggressive role in encouraging the Ethiopian government to uphold guarantees of press freedom. Secretary of State Christopher position is welcome step toward what CPJ hopes will be continued U.S. government commitment to press freedom and support for Ethiopia independent journalists said Kerina. We look forward to the time when the Ethiopian government views the private press as an ally not an enemy in the democratic process.
